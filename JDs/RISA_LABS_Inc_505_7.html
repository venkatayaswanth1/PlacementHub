Job Description

About RISA Labs At RISA, we are building the future of mission-critical workflows by leveraging the latest in AI. Our purpose is to solve humanity’s hardest hurdles, and we are starting with the incredibly high-stakes challenges within oncology. We’re not just selling software; we are delivering a new kind of resilient, autonomous infrastructure that powers foundational institutions and ultimately helps accelerate human progress. Founders RISA was founded by Kshitij Jaggi and Kumar Shivang, IIT Kanpur alumni with a proven track record from their previous healthcare startup, Urban Health. Their vision is to streamline oncology care through cutting-edge technology. Funding RISA Labs is backed by $3.5 million in seed funding, led by Flipkart co-founder Binny Bansal, with support from Oncology Ventures, General Catalyst, z21 Ventures, Odd Bird VC, and angel investor Ashish Gupta The Opportunity As a Technical Associate Product Manager (Technical APM) at RISA Labs, you’ll sit at the intersection of AI systems, backend infrastructure, and healthcare workflows - shaping how our technology is architected, delivered, and scaled in real-world oncology environments. You’ll work closely with engineering, AI/ML, and infra teams to translate complex clinical and operational requirements into robust technical specifications, APIs, and data workflows. You will be hands-on with the product’s technical underpinnings - able to dive into architecture discussions, understand tradeoffs, and help ensure we ship reliable, scalable systems that clinicians can trust. This role is ideal for someone who: ● Has a strong technical foundation and enjoys talking to engineers in their language ● Loves understanding system behavior in production just as much as UX flows ● Wants to see their technical decisions directly impact clinicians, hospitals, and ultimately, patients What You’ll do ● Drive the end-to-end product lifecycle - from problem definition and discovery to launch and iteration ● Work closely with engineering and AI teams to turn product concepts into reliable, scalable technical solutions (services, APIs, data flows, and workflows) ● Collaborate with clinical experts, users, and customers to deeply understand their workflows, technical constraints, and data requirements ● Define and own product requirements, success metrics, and roadmaps that align with business goals and technical feasibility ● Translate complex AI and technical concepts into simple, clear product narratives, user experiences, and technical specs that engineers can execute on ● Contribute to high-level system design conversations with engineering (e.g., integration points, data contracts, edge cases, performance considerations) ● Work with engineers to ensure products are instrumented for observability – metrics, logs, and basic monitoring - and use these to understand system behavior in production ● Partner with founders and leadership to shape product vision, strategy, and go-to-market readiness for technically sophisticated capabilities ● Constantly measure impact, gather feedback, and iterate - ensuring that every release moves us closer to our mission What You’ll Bring ● Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science, Engineering, Information Technology, or a related technical field ● Internships or projects in software engineering, AI/ML, product, UX, or system design are a plus ● Technically fluent and open to writing small amounts of code or scripts (for example, to explore APIs, inspect data, prototype ideas, or validate assumptions) ● Ability to read and reason about technical artifacts - architecture diagrams, API specs, and basic logs/metrics – and have productive, detailed conversations with engineers ● Strong analytical, problem-solving, and communication skills – you can connect the dots between user needs, technical feasibility, and business goals ● A builder’s mindset - comfortable working in ambiguity, taking ownership, and making data-informed decisions ● Curiosity for AI, healthcare, and how technology can make systems more human, efficient, and impactful ● A bias for action - you like moving fast, learning fast, and seeing tangible outcomes from your work
