Job Description

Job Description – Molecular Biology - Cell Line Development Position: Scientist for Molecular Biology Role Summary Expertise expected for developing, optimizing, and scaling up bioprocesses to produce. This role involves hands-on laboratory work, data analysis, and collaboration with other team members to ensure efficient and high-quality production. Key Responsibilities: 1. Process Development and Optimization • Assist in designing and performing experiments in fermentation, cell culture, and downstream processing. • Support the development of scalable and efficient bioprocesses. 2. Laboratory Techniques • Perform basic lab activities such as: o Media preparation and sterilization o Cell cultivation and maintenance (bacteria/yeast/mammalian) o Enzyme and biochemical assays o Chromatography and protein purification • Apply aseptic techniques in handling cultures. 3. Molecular Biology & Microbiology (as applicable to projects) • Learn and apply techniques such as: o DNA/RNA extraction, PCR, site-directed mutagenesis o Western blotting, SDS-PAGE, ELISA o Bacterial enumeration, culture maintenance, and pathogen identification 4. Bioinformatics (Basic) • Support data interpretation using tools for: o Primer designing, DNA sequencing analysis o Homology modeling, docking studies 5. Data Analysis • Compile and analyze experimental data using basic statistical tools. • Present findings in a clear and organized manner. 6. Equipment Handling • Assist in the operation and basic maintenance of lab equipment: o Bioreactors, centrifuges, spectrophotometers, chromatography units 7. Documentation • Maintain accurate lab notebooks and records of all experiments. • Prepare basic reports and summaries under guidance. 8. Teamwork & Communication • Collaborate with cross-functional teams (scientists, technicians, engineers). • Actively participate in team discussions and meetings. 9. Regulatory Awareness • Learn good laboratory practices (GLP), GMP fundamentals, and industry compliance guidelines. Skill Sets: • Education: Master’s degree (M.Sc., M.Tech, or equivalent) or a Doctoral degree (Ph.D.) in Biotechnology, Molecular Biology, Biochemistry, Microbiology, Biochemical Engineering, or a related field. • Experience: Freshers or candidates with academic projects, internships, or training in relevant bioprocess areas.
