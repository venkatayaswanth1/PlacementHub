Job Description

About the role: We are looking for experienced Research Engineers to expand our core team. The problems revolve around novel research on zero-shot solutions in automatic speech recognition, speech synthesis, prosody transfer, voice cloning, lip-sync to unseen faces, etc. You will get to own the core Chariot algorithms for these problems, and scale them for real world use cases. What you'll work on: • Designing, implementing, evaluating, deploying and iterating on speech synthesis models across languages and domains. • Researching and reproducing cutting-edge progress in Speech Synthesis, NLP and ML in academia and industry by staying abreast with new publications, conferences, and product applications. Minimum Qualifications: • Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science or related field. • Ability to generate and execute on research ideas. • Experience with Research Framework (PyTorch preferred). Preferred Qualifications: • Masters / Ph.D. in computer science or a related field. • Strong Publication Record at top tier Research and Artificial Intelligence conferences. • Experience with Speech Processing. Why should you join us? • Working very closely with the founding team, you also have the chance to influence and drive the direction of the company significantly. • Starting with coding/engineering solutions, you’ll have the opportunity to grow with the team and lead it. • Thrill of an early-stage startup growing rapidly. • Work directly on frontier-scale reasoning models. • Contribute to breakthroughs in text and speech AI. • Join a team backed by the IndiaAI Mission, working at true global scale. About Us: Chariot has been selected under the IndiaAI Mission to build reasoning-based frontier AI models from India, at a scale rarely attempted globally. Our infrastructure spans 1000+ GPUs, enabling trillion-token training runs. We are beginning with text and speech models, advancing reasoning capabilities in natural language understanding and conversational AI. We are now seeking to hire our first campus cohort of engineers — graduates who want to contribute to one of India’s most ambitious deep-tech efforts.
