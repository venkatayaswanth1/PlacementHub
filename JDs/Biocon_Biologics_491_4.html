Job Description – Analytical Sciences – Functional Characterisation Position: Scientist for Functional Characterisation Role Summary Exciting opportunity to work on cutting-edge biopharma projects involving method development, bioassays, and data analysis for therapeutic proteins like monoclonal antibodies and insulin analogues. Gain hands-on experience with advanced lab techniques (ELISA, Flow Cytometry, SPR/BLI), work with modern instruments, and contribute to real-world drug development under global guidelines. Ideal for candidates passionate about science, innovation, and teamwork. Key Responsibilities: • Method development/ qualification/ transfer, stability sample testing, CQA assessment, biosimilarity analysis of monoclonal antibodies, insulin analogues, pegylated proteins, antibody-drug conjugates, therapeutic peptides. • Knowledge on ICH, USP and WHO guidelines, systems biology, and its relevance to drug development in biopharmaceutical industry. • Aseptic technique of handling tissue culture model. • Thorough understanding of cell culture and hands-on experience with in vitro bioassay techniques and their applications • Knowledge on novel technologies (including but not limited to HTRF/AlphaLISA, Luminescence, ELISA, Flow Cytometry, SPR, BLI) • Handling instruments like (Plate Reader, Flow Cytometer, Biacore, Octet) and familiarization with 96, 384 well formats of assay • Interpret and analyse data using relevant instrument software • Maintain instruments in coordination with operations team and support installation of new instrument.
